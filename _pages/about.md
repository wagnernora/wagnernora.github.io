# Research Interests

My research focuses on several key areas within the field of computational astronomy:

## Galactic Dynamics
Investigating the movements and behavior of galaxies through various computational methods.

## Young Stellar Populations
Studying the characteristics and development of young stars within our galaxy and beyond.

## Master’s Thesis on Carina
Wagner's Master's thesis work concentrated on the Carina region, utilizing Gaia DR3 data to analyze stellar populations and their dynamics. This research offered significant insights into the structure and evolution of this fascinating star-forming region.

## Deep Clustering Algorithms
Nora has also contributed to the field by developing deep clustering algorithms aimed at identifying and studying co-moving groups of stars. This innovative work enhances our understanding of stellar associations and their formative processes.

## Contributions to Star and Planet Formation
Her contributions extend to understanding the mechanisms behind star and planet formation, shedding light on the conditions necessary for these processes to occur in various environments.
